# Style DNA from the six reference avatars

Use this file as the source of truth for visual consistency.

## Core impression

The shared look is a youthful stylized 3D social-profile avatar family.

It sits in a narrow middle zone:
- more dimensional than flat illustration
- more polished than a rough clay render
- less realistic than a beauty portrait
- less childish than a mascot or chibi toy
- more fashionable than a simple corporate icon, but not editorial

The references are cohesive because they balance cute and cool. They feel like profile avatars for young adults, not dolls, not children, and not runway photography.

They are also not sterile. Several references use one clear personal styling accent such as a hat, sunglasses, or earrings. Hair variation is also part of the family look; not everyone should collapse into black hair plus the same haircut.

## Stable visual traits

### 1. Rendering medium
- Stylized 3D render.
- Medium detail, not hyper-detailed.
- Surfaces are smooth and controlled.
- Skin is soft and clean, never pore-detailed.
- Hair is built from readable sculpted clumps.
- Clothing and accessories are simplified but tasteful.

### 2. Proportions and silhouette
- Head is prominent in the frame.
- Shoulders are present but cropped high and kept secondary.
- Neck is short to moderate, never elongated.
- Bust crop is compact, but not exaggerated bobblehead.
- Overall silhouette reads as a young-adult avatar portrait, not a toy figurine.

### 3. Facial construction
- Face shape is smooth and youthful.
- Eyes are larger than realistic, but still human-like.
- Eyelids are visible.
- Irises are readable when not hidden by tinted lenses.
- Brows are simple and graphic.
- Nose is tiny but still sculpted in 3D.
- Mouth is small and relaxed.
- Jawline is gentle, not sharp, but also not baby-round.
- Expressions stay mild: soft friendliness, cool neutral, quiet confidence, subtle smirk.

### 4. Hair language
- Hair is graphic and shape-driven.
- Use soft sculpted clumps and layered masses.
- Silhouettes are readable and stylish.
- Avoid realistic flyaways and salon strands.
- Avoid hard-edged blocky slabs that make the result look low-poly.
- Avoid helmet hair.
- Vary haircuts and hair colors across characters when the subject supports it.
- Favor natural or softly stylized hair colors that harmonize with the character mood.

### 5. Styling language
- Styling is modern and youthful.
- Clothing is intentionally reduced.
- One visible garment shape is enough.
- Accessories are often the strongest identity marker.
- Accessories can be slightly bold or oversized, but the result should still feel like a profile portrait rather than a toy costume.
- The feeling is casual stylish avatar, not editorial fashion pose and not corporate stock icon.
- Preserve one tasteful styling accent whenever possible.
- Good accent types in this family include hats, glasses, sunglasses, simple earrings, a subtle necklace, a tiny hair accessory, or one clean tech/fashion item.
- Keep accents readable but limited. Do not pile on props.

### 6. Camera and crop
- One subject only.
- Bust portrait, chest-up or slightly tighter.
- Slight 3/4 turn is the safest default.
- Eye-level camera or very slight high angle.
- Crop high enough that the body never becomes the focus.
- Avoid dead-center passport symmetry unless the user explicitly wants it.

### 7. Background system
- One clean solid pastel full-frame background behind the subject.
- Use a single plain color field filling the whole image.
- Do not use gradients.
- No room, scenery, furniture, particles, or story environment.
- Background tones stay pale and quiet.
- If a provided screenshot appears circular, treat that circle as a platform crop artifact, not as part of the original composition.

### 8. Lighting and materials
- Soft portrait lighting.
- Low contrast.
- Controlled highlights.
- No dramatic rim lighting, no cinematic shadows, no glossy beauty-ad shine.
- Skin reads smooth and softly lit.
- Hair reads satin-matte.
- Clothing reads simplified and secondary.
- Accessories may carry a slightly stronger color accent or material accent, but should still stay clean and stylized.

### 9. Output cleanliness
- Treat the avatar as outward-facing artwork, suitable for public profiles and presentations.
- Do not include watermarks, ai-generated labels, signatures, logos, captions, timestamps, or platform UI.
- Do not place any text inside the frame unless the user explicitly asks for it.

## Exclusions

Do not drift toward:
- photorealistic fashion portrait
- cosmetic beauty render
- toy figurine or vinyl doll
- low-poly mascot
- clay chibi icon
- corporate stock-avatar plainness
- over-decorated costume character
- noisy or scenic background
- repeated black-hair default for every subject
- repeated one-size-fits-all male haircut
